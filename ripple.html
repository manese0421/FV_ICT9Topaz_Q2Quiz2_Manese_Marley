what is ripple?

Ripple, established in 2012 by Ripple Labs, is a digital payment protocol known for its rapid and low-cost transactions across borders. Unlike other cryptocurrencies, Ripple's goal is to improve the efficiency of international money transactions by collaborating with banks and financial institutions.

key features of ripple

Feature Description
instant cross-border payments Ripple enables fast and cost-effective cross-border transactions, making it ideal for international payments.
partnerships with financial institutions Ripple collaborates with banks and payment providers worldwide to facilitate faster, more secure transactions.
unique consensus protocol Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, Ripple uses a consensus ledger rather than traditional blockchain mining.
